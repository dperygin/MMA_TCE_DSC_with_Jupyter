#Variable description
#Variables used in this notebook may differ from the standard notation for the purpose of clarity. 
#The recommended calculation for determining the Mass Change Rate (MCR) is performed according to the following formula
#ML = [(MS -MB)/MS]X100, where ML is the percent mass loss over the process (given by the variable rateOfMassLoss),
#MS is the starting mass for this process as defined by the intersection (A) (x_temp_i, y_mass_i), of the starting mass line (line1)
#and the maximum gradient tangent to the TG (thermogravimetric) curve (line2). MB (x_temp_f, y_mass_f) is the final mass resulting from this process,
#as calculated from the maximum gradient tangent to the TG curve (line2) and the horizontal line formed after the process
#is complete (line3). MS and MB are not used here to calculate ML. The mass of the nearest neighbor to each on the original curve 
#is calculated as real_y_mass_f and real_x_temp_f and used for these points.
